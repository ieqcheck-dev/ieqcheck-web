Building owners and developers aiming for BREEAM, WELL or LEED certification often face the challenge of demonstrating compliance with strict indoor air quality (IAQ) standards. Nitrous Oxide (Entonox) Concerns in Hospitals

Nitrous oxide, commonly known as Entonox, is widely used in hospitals for pain relief, particularly in areas like maternity suites. However, improper ventilation or leaks can lead to the build-up of nitrous oxide in the air, posing potential long-term health risks to staff who are regularly exposed. Prolonged exposure to elevated levels of nitrous oxide can cause dizziness, nausea, and even neurological effects, making it essential to monitor and control its concentration in hospital environments.

Have a question? Check out the FAQ

What is indoor air quality (IAQ), and why is it important?

Indoor air quality refers to the air quality within and around buildings. Poor IAQ can impact health, comfort, and well-being by exposing individuals to pollutants.

What pollutants can be found in indoor air?

Common pollutants include carbon monoxide, carbon dioxide, volatile organic compounds (VOCs), formaldehyde, airborne particles (PM2.5 & PM10), and biological contaminants like mold and bacteria.

How do you measure indoor air quality?

We use specialized monitoring equipment to measure gases, airborne particles, humidity, temperature, and ventilation rates to assess IAQ.

What are the signs of poor indoor air quality?

Symptoms include headaches, dizziness, fatigue, respiratory issues, and unpleasant odors.

Can indoor air quality affect health?

Yes, prolonged exposure to poor IAQ can lead to respiratory diseases, allergies, and long-term health effects.

How can I improve my indoor air quality?

Regular ventilation, air purifiers, reducing pollutant sources, and professional IAQ assessments can help improve air quality.
